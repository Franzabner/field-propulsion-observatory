# Public Safety Language

Use language that preserves uncertainty and avoids unsupported propulsion claims.

## Replacement Language

| Instead of | Say |
| --- | --- |
| "This proves anti-gravity." | "The available public data is insufficient to evaluate whether the apparent motion reflects physical acceleration, sensor artifact, or range ambiguity." |
| "Zero-point craft." | "A speculative field-propulsion hypothesis would require evidence of calibrated acceleration, power behavior, and heat management beyond known systems." |
| "Aliens are confirmed." | "The record does not support a conclusion about origin." |
| "The government is hiding the propulsion system." | "The public evidence package is incomplete and cannot support a propulsion conclusion." |
| "It moved impossibly fast." | "The apparent angular motion may imply high speed under some range assumptions; range must be bounded before speed can be claimed." |

## Required Terms

- Apparent motion.
- Declared assumptions.
- Range-dependent estimate.
- Missing metadata.
- Known-cause screening.
- Hypothesis boundary.
- Unresolved.

## Prohibited Tone

Avoid sensational, conspiratorial, spiritual, science-fiction, or propaganda-coded framing. The repo should be serious enough for aerospace engineers, technical writers, scientific reviewers, simulation engineers, and public-interest researchers.
