# Positioning

`field-propulsion-observatory` is an engineering framework for turning anomalous motion claims into testable sensor, trajectory, and propulsion questions.

Detection is not resolution.

Modern sensor civilization can produce unresolved observations without producing public scientific closure. Cameras, infrared sensors, radar tracks, platform motion, compression, clock drift, zoom, and partial metadata can create observations that are difficult to classify from public artifacts alone.

## Grounding Principles

1. Detection is not resolution.
2. Video is not enough without metadata.
3. Apparent motion is not proven motion until range, frame, sensor, and timing are known.
4. Unresolved does not mean extraterrestrial.
5. Extraordinary propulsion hypotheses require calibrated multi-sensor evidence.
6. The repo studies the evaluation process, not secret claims.
7. Field propulsion and zero-point energy are speculative hypothesis vocabulary, not demonstrated technology.
8. Simulation does not prove an event happened; simulation tests what the evidence would imply under stated assumptions.
9. Unknown range creates unknown size, speed, acceleration, and energy.
10. No propulsion conclusion should be made from video alone.

## Public Technical Surface

This repo is intended to strengthen Franzabner's public surface as a mechanical, electrical, and autonomous systems architect who can design evidence pipelines, simulation boundaries, and public-safe technical documentation without unsupported claims.

## Working Question

When an object appears to move beyond conventional aerospace expectations, what data would be required to know whether the motion is real, misread, explainable, simulated under assumptions, or unresolved?

## Boundary

The repository does not prove exotic propulsion. It defines the reasoning discipline required before any propulsion language can be responsibly considered.
