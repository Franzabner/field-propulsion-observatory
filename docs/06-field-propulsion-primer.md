# Field Propulsion Primer

Field propulsion is treated here as a speculative concept. Instead of pushing mass backward, a field-coupled vehicle would hypothetically alter its interaction with the surrounding medium, inertia, or geometry.

## Conventional Propulsion

Conventional propulsion exchanges momentum with mass or fields through known mechanisms: propellers, jets, rockets, rotors, wheels, sails, electromagnetic drives in prepared environments, or reaction control systems.

## Field Interaction

A field-interaction hypothesis would require measurable coupling between a vehicle, its environment, and a force-producing field. Public claims require more than apparent motion; they require calibrated evidence of the field interaction and its effects.

## Drag Reduction

Drag-reduction concepts can include boundary-layer control, plasma effects, shape optimization, or flow manipulation. Reduced drag is not propulsion by itself and does not explain energy source, control, or acceleration.

## Inertia Question

Claims about inertia management require precise acceleration, mass, structural load, and energy accounting. Apparent acceleration from video is not enough.

## Heat Rejection Problem

Any high-power system must account for heat. A field-propulsion hypothesis must explain power source, conversion losses, thermal signature, and heat rejection.

## Control Problem

Motion requires controlled force direction, stability, and feedback. A speculative hypothesis must describe control authority and observable consequences.

## Evidence Problem

Without calibrated multi-sensor evidence, public artifacts can at most motivate questions. They cannot establish field propulsion.
