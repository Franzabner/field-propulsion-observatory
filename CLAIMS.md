# Claims

## Allowed Claims

- This repository is a public-safe engineering framework for evaluating anomalous motion claims.
- The repository uses schemas, synthetic examples, documentation, and lightweight simulation to test reasoning pipelines.
- Simulation can show how assumptions about range, timing, sensor state, and platform motion affect apparent speed or acceleration.
- Unresolved is a valid result when evidence is insufficient.
- Field propulsion and zero-point energy are treated as speculative hypothesis vocabulary, not demonstrated public propulsion technology.

## Forbidden Claims

This repository must not claim:

- aliens confirmed;
- extraterrestrial technology;
- zero-point propulsion proven;
- anti-gravity proven;
- reverse-engineered craft;
- recovered craft or recovered materials;
- classified programs;
- free energy;
- usable zero-point power;
- confirmed exotic propulsion;
- government coverups;
- real classified case conclusions;
- real case accusations;
- named real pilot, whistleblower, or disputed-event conclusions;
- released Hugging Face dataset, model, Space, or report claims unless reviewed artifacts actually exist;
- live Upwork service claims unless separately reviewed and approved.

## Required Boundary Language

No propulsion conclusion should be made from video alone. Extraordinary propulsion hypotheses require calibrated multi-sensor evidence, declared assumptions, reconstructable geometry, and reviewable metadata.

## Release-Claim Rule

Status language must stay factual:

- The repo may say `scaffolded` while it remains a prepared publication candidate.
- It may say `planned-only` for Hugging Face until an approved artifact exists.
- It may say `draft-only` for Upwork/service material until reviewed publication copy exists.
- It must not imply public release, model release, dataset release, Space deployment, real-case conclusion, or client service availability without evidence and human review.
